## Known issues
- **ARCHITECTURE FINDINGS (Proof 2, see `PLANETARY_PROOF.md`):**
  - 2a: a double-precision integrator correctly proves the gravity MODEL — body released above the equator falls radially (−X, zero Y/Z drift) and rests on the real surface with `up=(1,0,0)`.
  - 2b: stock Chaos at ~6.37e8 cm from origin fails (5× too slow, drifts outward, slides without resting) — large-world float precision.
  - 2c: floating origin (`AFloatingOriginManager`) WORKS (rebasing confirmed, body held at origin), but the Chaos body STILL won't fall — its world position is ejected ~985 cm/s while physics velocity reads ~10, i.e. the body is shoved out by glitchy contacts with the **Earth-sized single static collision mesh**. Floating origin fixes the body's coordinates but the planet is still one huge far-centered mesh.
  - **2d: the local patch is built and correct, but Chaos `AddForce` itself is the wall.** With `ProofEarth` set QueryOnly and a clean `LocalSurfacePatch` 1 km below, the body STILL won't fall at planetary scale (drifts outward ~97 cm/s, reported velocity ~0). A near-origin control (gravity center AT origin) shows it falls radially but **~100× too slow**: position-derived a≈9.8 cm/s² vs commanded 980, with reported velocity itself 10× low (a≈96). So `Mesh->AddForce(dir*g, bAccelChange=true)` is quantitatively broken even at the origin — it is not (only) a large-coordinate problem.
- **Necessary architecture (now PROVEN by 2e, not just hypothesized):** the planet cannot be one giant collision mesh — use LOCAL collision near origin (`LocalSurfacePatch`), and drive the fall/movement by **direct double-precision integration + swept movement** (`ASweptGravityBody`), NOT Chaos `AddForce`. 2e demonstrates a body falling and resting correctly this way. NOTE: because sweeps are queries, the giant `ProofEarth` must be `NoCollision` (even `QueryOnly` blocks swept queries at planetary scale); local patches are the only colliders.
- **PIE gotcha (2e):** `static_mesh_component.set_collision_enabled(NO_COLLISION)` at edit time does NOT reliably survive PIE/Simulate duplication — a marker set NoCollision still blocked the swept body in PIE. To exclude an actor from a physics test, DESTROY it in setup (or change its saved collision profile), don't just toggle collision_enabled.
- **Open sub-question (does not block the controller):** the 2d ~100× `AddForce` deficit is still unexplained. Captured the project physics settings (`_authoring/dump_physics_settings.py` → `Saved/PhysicsSettings.json`): `max_physics_delta_time=1/30`, `max_substep_delta_time=1/60`, `max_substeps=6`, `default_terminal_velocity=4000`, `default_gravity_z=-980`. These RULE OUT terminal-velocity capping (4000 cm/s was never reached) and a slow-motion artifact (the body's `Elapsed` tracked wall-clock ~1:1, so Simulate ran at real-time, not low-fps). So in real-time the commanded 980 cm/s² produced only ~9.8 cm/s² of actual motion — a clean ÷100 that the settings alone do not explain. Next diagnostic: a runtime per-tick probe (log `DeltaSeconds` and the position/velocity delta each tick — needs a small C++ logging add) or how `AddForce(bAccelChange)` interacts with Chaos substepping. The custom integrator sidesteps Chaos forces entirely, so it does not block the build.
