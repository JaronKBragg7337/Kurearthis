- **ARCHITECTURE FINDINGS (Proof 2, see `PLANETARY_PROOF.md`):**
  - 2a: a double-precision integrator correctly proves the gravity MODEL ‚Äî body released above the equator falls radially (‚àíX, zero Y/Z drift) and rests on the real surface with `up=(1,0,0)`.
  - 2b: stock Chaos at ~6.37e8 cm from origin fails (5√ó too slow, drifts outward, slides without resting) ‚Äî large-world float precision.
  - 2c: floating origin (`AFloatingOriginManager`) WORKS (rebasing confirmed, body held at origin), but the Chaos body STILL won't fall ‚Äî its world position is ejected ~985 cm/s while physics velocity reads ~10, i.e. the body is shoved out by glitchy contacts with the **Earth-sized single static collision mesh**. Floating origin fixes the body's coordinates but the planet is still one huge far-centered mesh.
  - **2d: the local patch is built and correct, but Chaos `AddForce` itself is the wall.** With `ProofEarth` set QueryOnly and a clean `LocalSurfacePatch` 1 km below, the body STILL won't fall at planetary scale (drifts outward ~97 cm/s, reported velocity ~0). A near-origin control (gravity center AT origin) shows it falls radially but **~100√ó too slow**: position-derived a‚âà9.8 cm/s¬≤ vs commanded 980, with reported velocity itself 10√ó low (a‚âà96). So `Mesh->AddForce(dir*g, bAccelChange=true)` is quantitatively broken even at the origin ‚Äî it is not (only) a large-coordinate problem.
- **Necessary architecture (evidence-backed, NOT a shortcut):** the planet cannot be one giant static collision mesh ‚Äî use LOCAL collision near the origin (the `LocalSurfacePatch`, now built). And the fall/movement must be driven by **direct integration in a custom radial-gravity controller** (set velocity / sweep against the patch), NOT by Chaos `AddForce`, which 2d shows is unreliable. This mirrors 2a (the double-precision integrator fell in 14 s and rested exactly; Chaos forces do not). The custom controller is required, not optional.
- **Open sub-question (does not block the controller):** the 2d ~100√ó `AddForce` deficit is still unexplained. Captured the project physics settings (`_authoring/dump_physics_settings.py` ‚Üí `Saved/PhysicsSettings.json`): `max_physics_delta_time=1/30`, `max_substep_delta_time=1/60`, `max_substeps=6`, `default_terminal_velocity=4000`, `default_gravity_z=-980`. These RULE OUT terminal-velocity capping (4000 cm/s was never reached) and a slow-motion artifact (the body's `Elapsed` tracked wall-clock ~1:1, so Simulate ran at real-time, not low-fps). So in real-time the commanded 980 cm/s¬≤ produced only ~9.8 cm/s¬≤ of actual motion ‚Äî a clean √∑100 that the settings alone do not explain. Next diagnostic: a runtime per-tick probe (log `DeltaSeconds` and the position/velocity delta each tick ‚Äî needs a small C++ logging add) or how `AddForce(bAccelChange)` interacts with Chaos substepping. The custom integrator sidesteps Chaos forces entirely, so it does not block the build.
